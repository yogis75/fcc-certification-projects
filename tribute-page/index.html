Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose

Great nationalist and freedom figher

Here's a time line of Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose

  • 1897 - Subhas Chandra Bose was born in Cuttack, Orissa Division, Bengal Province, to Prabhavati Dutt Bose and Janakinath Bose.
  • 1919 - He had cleared the Indian Civil Services (ICS) examination. Bose, however, resigned later.
  • 1921 - He met M.K. Gandhi. Gandhi directed Bose to work with Chittaranjan Das. He considered C.R.Das as his political mentor. Bose took over the editorship of the newspaper Forward founded by Chittaranjan Das's Swaraj Party.
  • 1925 - 27 - Due to C.R.Das's connection with revolutionary movements, Bose was sent to Mandalay prison in 1925 where he contracted Tuberculosis and he was released in 1927 due to his failing health.
  • 1930 - 1931 - He actively participated in the Salt Satyagraha of 1930 and vehemently opposed the suspension of Civil Disobedience Movement and signing of the Gandhi-Irwin Pact in 1931.
  • 1938 - He won the congress presidential elections at Haripura.
  • 1939 - Again at Tripuri, he won the presidential elections against Gandhi's candidate Pattabhi Sitarammayya. Due to ideological differences with Gandhi, Bose resigned and left congress. Rajendra Prasad was appointed in his place.
  • 1940 - When INC organized Individual Satyagraha, Subhas Chandra Bose organized an 'Anti-Compromise Conference' at Ramgarh, Bihar. He was arrested by the British shortly afterwards for organizing a protest for the removal of the Holwell Monument (Memorium for Blackhole tragedy of Calcutta). He was later released and placed under House arrest in Calcutta.
  • 1941 - In January, he made a dramatic escape disguised as a Muslim, and after a long detour reached Berlin. He founded the Free India Centre in Berlin and created the Indian Legion out of the prisoners of war who had previously fought for the British in North Africa prior to their capture by Axis forces.
  • 1942 - He was given the honorific title of Netaji in Germany by the Indian soldiers of the Indian Legion and officials of the Special Bureau for India in Berlin.
  • 1943 - He reached Japanese-controlled Singapore from Germany in July, issued from there his famous call, 'Delhi Chalo', and announced the formation of the Azad Hind Government and the Indian National Army on 21st October.
  • 1944 - The INA fought allied forces inside the borders of India in Imphal and in Burma.
  • 1945 - As the INA forces were being captured or surrendering, Bose travelled to Tokyo through Taiwan. Bose was reportedly killed in an air crash over Taipeh, Taiwan (Formosa) on August 18 (Although it is widely believed that he was still alive after the plane crash, not much information is available concerning this).
